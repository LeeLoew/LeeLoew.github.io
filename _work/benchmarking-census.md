## The context

Membership organizations usually see only their member file, a partial and
self-selected view of the market they serve. The question that matters is
the whole profession: everyone licensed in the state, member or not.

## The method

A full-population portrait built from public licensure data and governed by
a strict privacy-containment model: a hard wall between public and internal
data, aggregate-only reporting, and small-cell suppression so no individual
is ever exposed. The result is a view of an entire state profession, its
generations, its geography, and its membership penetration, built from data
anyone could legally access and handled more carefully than required.

## What it demonstrates

The method repeats in any state with an accessible licensure roster, which
makes it a template for membership organizations that want to see their
whole market rather than the slice that already joined. Privacy containment
is built into the method itself, so the rigor doesn't depend on anyone's
good intentions.
