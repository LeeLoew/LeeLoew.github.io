## The context

Membership organizations usually see only their member file — a partial,
self-selected view of the market they serve. The real question is the whole
profession: everyone licensed in the state, member or not.

## The method

A full-population portrait built from public licensure data, governed by a
disciplined privacy-containment model: a hard wall between public and
internal data, aggregate-only reporting, and small-cell suppression so no
individual is ever exposed. The result is a first-of-its-kind view of an
entire state profession — its generations, geography, and membership
penetration — built entirely from data anyone could legally access, handled
more carefully than anyone would require.

## What it demonstrates

Rigor and responsibility as method, not disclaimer. The approach repeats in
any state with an accessible licensure roster, which makes it a template for
membership organizations that want to see their whole market — not just the
slice that already joined.
