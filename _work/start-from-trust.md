## The challenge

Small and mid-size accounting firms were hearing the same advice from every
direction: adopt AI faster. The advice ignored the defining trait of the
profession, a deliberate and well-placed skepticism. Firms needed a way to
adopt technology on their own terms, without dismissing AI and without
over-trusting it.

## The approach

I built the package around a reframe: treat professional caution as an asset
and design adoption around it. The anchor is a primary research paper,
supported by tools a firm can put to work the same week: a vendor-evaluation
checklist, a where-do-I-start guide, a discussion guide for firm leaders,
and a quick-start list of actions.

At the center is the **AI Trust &amp; Usage Quadrant**, which maps people on
two axes: how much they use AI, and how much they trust it. Each quadrant is
a different starting point, and each needs a different first move.

## The results

The package shipped as a published report with supporting tools, blog
content, infographics, and video. Six state CPA organizations license and
rebrand it for their members, and a companion one-hour CPE webinar built
from the research runs on the national state-society circuit.

## What I learned

The reframe did the heavy lifting. Research that treats skepticism as
professional judgment gets a hearing that another adopt-faster report never
would. Firms listened because the work respected how they already make
decisions.
