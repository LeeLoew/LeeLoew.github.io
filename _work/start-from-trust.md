## The challenge

Small and mid-size accounting firms were being told, from every direction, to
"adopt AI faster." The advice ignored the defining trait of the profession:
deliberate, well-placed skepticism. Firms didn't need another hype deck — they
needed a way to adopt technology on their own terms, without either dismissing
AI or over-trusting it.

## The approach

I built a complete research package around a reframe: professional caution is
not a lag to overcome — it's a strength to build on. The anchor is a primary
research paper, supported by a set of tools a firm can actually use: a
vendor-evaluation checklist, a "Where do I start?" guide, a discussion guide
for firm leaders, and a quick-start list of actions for the coming week.

At the center is the **AI Trust & Usage Quadrant** — my framework mapping
where people sit on two axes, how much they *use* AI and how much they *trust*
it. Each quadrant is a different starting point needing a different
intervention: the over-trusting experimenter needs guardrails; the capable
skeptic needs a low-stakes win. Treating them identically is how rollouts
produce risk and resentment at once.

## The results

The package shipped as a published report with supporting tools, blog
content, infographics, and video. Six state CPA organizations license and
rebrand it for their members. A companion one-hour CPE webinar, built from
the research, runs on the national state-society circuit.

## What I learned

The reframe did the heavy lifting. Meeting a skeptical audience where it
stands — and treating that skepticism as professional judgment rather than
resistance — earned the research a hearing that another "adopt faster" report
never would have gotten. Designing for trust is the adoption strategy.
