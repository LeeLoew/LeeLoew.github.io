<div class="feature-item">
  <h3>The Missing 62% — AI membership intelligence at scale</h3>
  <p class="where">PICPA · Applied AI, built solo</p>
  <p>Pennsylvania's CPA association could see that a majority of the state's licensed CPAs — roughly 16,000 people — weren't members, but the prospect list was just names and license numbers. Researching them by hand, done properly, is an estimated <strong>three to four person-years of work</strong>. I built a four-stage multi-agent AI pipeline (anchor → corroborate → weigh → verify) that runs locally — sensitive data never leaves the building — and is designed to <em>decline rather than guess</em>: every field carries a source and a confidence level.</p>
  <p>The result: thousands of records enriched with a <strong>zero-wrong accuracy audit</strong>, outperforming a commercial data tool head-to-head (7/10 with zero errors vs. the vendor's 2–3/10, confidently wrong) — behind a <strong>seven-figure membership opportunity</strong>. Built solo on a flat ~$200/month AI subscription: no data-vendor contract, no added headcount.</p>
</div>

<div class="feature-item">
  <h3>Autonomous scenario forecasting</h3>
  <p class="where">Membership strategy · Methodology highlight</p>
  <p>A cohort-replacement membership forecast designed, built, and quality-gated by an autonomous overnight multi-agent AI run — including a two-model bake-off judged independently, backtesting to <strong>sub-1% error</strong> on the full sample, and an interactive scenario simulator leadership can use to test recruitment, retention, and pricing levers. AI-run quantitative strategy work with the rigor of a research lab.</p>
</div>

<div class="feature-item">
  <h3>Profession-wide benchmarking census</h3>
  <p class="where">Research methodology · Repeatable in any state</p>
  <p>The first full-population portrait of a state's accounting profession, built from public licensure data with a disciplined privacy-containment model (aggregate-only reporting, small-cell suppression, and a hard wall between public and internal data). A repeatable method for membership organizations that want to see their whole market — not just their member file.</p>
</div>

## What this work shows

- **Applied AI with measurable outcomes** — production pipelines, not proofs of concept
- **Trust-first design** — systems that cite sources, quantify confidence, and refuse to guess
- **Responsible by default** — privacy-first execution and explicit guardrails, demonstrated rather than claimed
- **Research & strategy craft** — scenario planning, benchmarking, survey programs, and competitive analysis that hold up to executive scrutiny
