My outside work takes three shapes: speaking, advisory, and research. The
common thread is evidence. Everything I present or recommend comes from
deployments I've run myself or research I've published, and my home field is
professional services, where the audience is skilled, busy, and rightly
skeptical of new tools.

## How I help

### Speaking & workshops

Keynotes, executive briefings, CPE webinars, and hands-on workshops on
applied AI and the behavioral science of adoption. I'm comfortable in front
of skeptical rooms; accountants trained me well. My current talk, built from
published research, runs on the national state-society circuit.

### Advisory

A thinking partner for leaders in the middle of an AI strategy. The usual
questions: where the real use cases are, why usage has stalled, how to design
for trust instead of mandating usage, and what responsible adoption should
look like for your people and your clients. I'm most useful when there's a
specific decision on the table.

### Research programs

Mixed-methods research built to support a decision: AI adoption and impact
studies, scenario planning and forecasting, benchmarking, and member or
customer research programs. I design, run, and report these end to end.

## Who this is for

Executives and teams in associations, professional services, and finance, or
any organization where AI has to earn the trust of skilled people before it
delivers value. If that sounds like your people, we'll get along.
